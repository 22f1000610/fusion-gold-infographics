The Feedstock Dilemma

The single greatest variable is the cost of the Mercury-198 feedstock. Currently a scientific curiosity, its price must plummet for fusion gold to be viable.

For the process to be economical, the price of industrial-scale Mercury-198 must decrease by a factor of 150 million.

Primary Hurdles & Uncertainties

Beyond the costs, the path to commercial fusion gold is fraught with immense technical, economic, and regulatory challenges that must be overcome.

Technology Readiness

Commercial fusion is still decades away. The gold transmutation process itself is at an extremely low Technology Readiness Level (TRL) and requires massive R&D investment.

Capital & Market Risk

Fusion plants require tens of billions in upfront capital. Furthermore, successfully producing large amounts of gold could devalue the commodity, undermining the entire economic model.

Regulatory Framework

No regulations exist for commercial fusion, let alone for handling and selling transmuted, initially radioactive materials. This uncertainty is a major barrier to development.
